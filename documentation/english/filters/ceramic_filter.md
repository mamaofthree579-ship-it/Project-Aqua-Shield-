# Low-Resource Ceramic Water Filter (Clay + Sawdust)
A simple ceramic microfilter that removes sediments and many bacteria.

---

## Materials
- Clay soil
- Fine sawdust, rice hulls, or straw fibers
- Water
- Mold (any bowl or container-shaped object)
- A place to fire the pot (kiln, metal barrel, clay oven)

---

## Instructions
### Step 1 — Mix Material
- Combine **3 parts clay** + **1 part sawdust**.
- Add water until mixture is firm like bread dough.

### Step 2 — Shape It
- Press mixture into a bowl or pot shape.
- Ensure walls ~1 cm thick.

### Step 3 — Dry
- Air dry in shade for 2–3 days.

### Step 4 — Fire
- Fire at **850–900°C** until hardened.
- Sawdust burns out → leaves pores that filter water.

### Optional
- Coat inside with **colloidal silver** if available.

---

## How to Use
1. Place ceramic pot into a clean container.
2. Pour water into pot.
3. Clean water drips through pores.

---

## Limitations
Does *not* remove:
- chemicals  
- heavy metals  
- salt  

Still recommended to disinfect water after filtration.
