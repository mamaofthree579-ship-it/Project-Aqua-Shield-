# Basic Gravity Micro-Bio Sand + Charcoal Filter
Low-cost, crisis-zone appropriate filtration system. Uses universal materials and requires no electricity.

---

## Materials Needed
- 1 clean plastic bottle (1–2 L)
- 1 piece of clean cloth or bandana
- 1 cup fine sand (washed if possible)
- 1/2 cup small gravel or pebbles
- 1/2 cup wood charcoal (crushed, NOT coal)
- String or rubber band
- Knife or scissors
- Clean container to catch filtered water

---

## Schematic (Text Blueprint)
Bottle is used upside-down.

**Layer order (top → bottom / where water exits):**
1. Crushed charcoal
2. Fine sand
3. Small gravel
4. Cloth tied over bottle neck
5. Filtered water drips into clean container

---

## Assembly Instructions
### Step 1 — Prepare the Bottle
1. Wash bottle.
2. Cut off bottom end (this becomes the opening you pour water into).
3. Remove cap.
4. Cover bottle neck with clean cloth.
5. Tie cloth securely.

### Step 2 — Add Layers
1. Add 1/2 cup small gravel (bottom layer).
2. Add 1 cup fine sand (middle).
3. Add 1/2 cup crushed charcoal (top).

---

## First Use / Activation
Pour 1–2 liters of water through and discard the first batch.
This removes dust and settles layers.

---

## Daily Use
- Pour water slowly into the top opening.
- Allow gravity to drip water into a clean container.
- Drip rate is normal.

---

## Safety Note
For safe drinking:
**FILTER + disinfect**
- boil 1 minute  
- OR disinfect with chlorine tablets  
- OR use unscented bleach (1 drop per 200 mL)

---

## Maintenance
- Weekly → replace charcoal, rinse sand lightly.
- Monthly → replace sand & gravel.
- Replace full filter every 2–3 months or if moldy.
